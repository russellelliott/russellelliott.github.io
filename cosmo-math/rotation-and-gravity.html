Important Details

Siderial v. Syndonic Periods
  • The syndonic rotational period is different than the siderial period of a planet or other cellestial body. The siderial period is how long it takes a body to compelte a spin on its axis.
  • The syndonic period is how long it takes for the sun to appear in the same place in the sky, the length of a day.
  • A sidereal day is 23 hours 56 minutes and 4.09 seconds long. A syndonic day is 24 hours long.
